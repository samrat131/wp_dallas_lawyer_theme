About Walker Fults

Walker Fults

Walker Fults was born and raised in Dallas, Texas, where he graduated from St. Mark’s School of Texas, cum laude. He attended Duke University, where he published a law review article about domestic warrantless surveillance before the issue was brought to mainstream attention by The New York Times. He attended law school at the University of Virginia, where he participated in the selective criminal defense clinic, and was the only practitioner to obtain a not guilty verdict at trial for a client charged with a serious drug crime.

Walker Fults has worked as a visiting scholar for International Justice Mission’s office in Mumbai, India, where he collaborated closely with local attorneys to combat sexual trafficking and prostitution of minors. He also instructed over two hundred university students in English courses during a year at a university in China. He has traveled to over 30 different countries, and is conversational in Spanish and Chinese.

After law school, he was selected to clerk for the United States District Court for the Southern District of Texas, where he authored over twenty-five draft orders and opinions disposing of a wide range of federal cases related to civil rights litigation, qualified immunity, immigration, and judicial fraud, among other subjects. After his federal clerkship, he returned to Dallas, where he worked for several years at top private law firms. He has gone on to open his own law practice, focused on bringing the best legal representation to those who need it most.
